# Forged Landscapes — Photography Art Direction

_One page. The goal: every real project shot looks like it belongs to the same premium brand — even taken on a phone. Shared across both identity lanes._

## The look in one line
**Deep greens, soft light, straight lines, no people, matte finish.** Editorial, not estate-agent.

## Light — the single biggest lever
- Shoot in the **golden hour** (first hour after sunrise / last hour before sunset) **or** under **bright overcast** cloud.
- **Never** shoot in harsh midday sun — it blows out paving and crushes the greens into black shadows.
- Keep the sun **behind you or to the side**, never pointing at the lens.

## Composition
- **Two hero angles only**, so the set stays consistent:
  1. **Straight-on** — camera square to the patio/wall, horizon dead level.
  2. **Three-quarter** — stand at a corner, shoot diagonally across the space to show depth.
- **Level horizons.** Turn on your phone's grid (Settings → Camera → Grid) and line the top of a fence or wall to it.
- **Lead with the craft:** the edge of the paving, the joint lines, the fall of the steps. Get low — phone at knee height — to make hard landscaping feel architectural.
- **People-free hero shots.** Furniture, planting and light do the talking. (Keep a few candid team/process shots separately for social — not for the main gallery.)
- Shoot **wide first, then three detail shots** of every job: a joint/edge close-up, a material texture, one planting detail.

## Before / after (the audit's priority)
- Shoot the **before** the moment you arrive, from a **fixed spot** — mark it (a paving slab, a drain). Shoot the **after from the exact same spot**, same height, same lens. That match is what makes the pair sell.
- Same time of day if you can, so the light matches.

## Framing & format
- **Landscape (horizontal)** for heroes and before/afters. One consistent crop: **3:2**.
- One **square (1:1)** and one **vertical 4:5** of the best detail for Instagram.
- Fill the frame with the work — trim sky and neighbouring fences.

## Phone settings
- **Highest resolution**, HDR **on**, flash **off**, grid **on**.
- Tap to focus on the **mid-ground** (the patio), then drag the exposure slider **down a touch** to protect highlights.
- Wipe the lens. Hold still — brace against a wall or use a cheap tripod.

## The grade (editing) — keep it matte and green
Free apps: Lightroom Mobile or Snapseed. One preset, applied to everything:
- **Highlights down, shadows up** slightly — recover detail, no crushed blacks.
- **Contrast:** gentle. **Clarity/Texture:** +10–15 on hard landscaping only.
- **Saturation down ~5, but greens up** — deep, natural, not neon.
- **Warmth:** nudge slightly cool for overcast, slightly warm for golden hour.
- **No filters, no vignettes, no fake bokeh.** The finish is **matte** — pull highlights off pure white.

## Do / Don't
| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Golden hour / soft overcast | Harsh midday sun |
| Level horizons, straight lines | Tilted "dynamic" angles |
| Tidy site first — sweep, coil hoses | Tools, vans, bins, cables in shot |
| Same spot for before/after | Different angle for the "after" |
| Deep, natural greens | Oversaturated, HDR-cartoon look |
| People-free heroes | Client's washing / cars in frame |

## Delivery
- Keep **originals** (don't only save the edited copy).
- Name files `postcode_service_angle` (e.g. `WD17_patio_straight.jpg`).
- Aim for **3–6 finished pairs** before pushing the service×town pages — proof first, then traffic.
