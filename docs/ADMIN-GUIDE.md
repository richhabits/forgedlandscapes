# Running Forged Landscapes — the admin console

Everything you need to run the business day-to-day lives at **one web address**:

> **forgedlandscapes.co.uk/admin**

Bookmark it on your phone. You never need to open Supabase, spreadsheets, or
anything technical. If you can read email, you can run this.

---

## Signing in (no passwords, ever)

1. Go to **/admin** on your phone or laptop.
2. Type your email, tap **Send link**.
3. Open the email from Forged Landscapes and tap the link. You're in.

The link signs you in for a good while, so most days you'll go straight to the
inbox. There's nothing to remember and nothing to lose. (First time only: the
owner adds your email to the admin list — a one-off setup step in `SETUP.md`.)

---

## The dashboard — the top strip

Five numbers, this week versus last:

| Tile | What it tells you |
|------|-------------------|
| **New leads** | Enquiries that arrived this week, and whether that's up or down |
| **Briefs submitted** | Clients who finished the full project brief in the portal |
| **Assessor → brief** | How many people who used the online assessor went on to send a brief |
| **Out of area** | Enquiries from beyond the 20-mile radius — your evidence for whether it's worth expanding |
| **Median 1st response** | How quickly, on average, you act on a new lead |

Nothing to click — it's just the pulse of the week at a glance.

---

## The inbox — every enquiry, in one list

Below the numbers is every lead. Each row shows the name, what they want, their
budget, postcode, distance, when it came in, and where it came from (assessor,
form, etc.). A coloured **status tag** tells you where they are in the pipeline —
**gold tags mean it needs your attention**.

**To find things:**
- **Search box** — type a name, email or postcode.
- **All / In area / Out** — show only local jobs, or only out-of-area ones.
- **Status chips** (New, Qualified, Quoted…) — tap one to show just those, with a count.
- **List / columns icons** — switch between a simple list and a pipeline board.
- **CSV** — downloads whatever you're currently looking at as a spreadsheet.

## Opening a lead

Tap any lead. A panel slides in with the full picture:

- **Call** and **Email** buttons — tap Call to phone them straight away.
- Their full details: postcode with distance from base, project, budget, timeline, message.
- **The assessor conversation**, if they used it — read exactly what they told the robot.
- A link to their **full brief**, if they've submitted one (see below).

### Three things you'll do here

1. **Advance the status.** One gold button moves them to the next stage — e.g.
   *Advance → Qualified*. Tap it as you work a lead through to *Won*.
2. **Send a ready-made reply.** Three buttons, one tap each:
   - **Book the 15-min call** — invites them to a quick call.
   - **Offer survey slots** — moves toward a site visit.
   - **Out-of-area waitlist** — politely tells someone too far away they're on the list.
   
   (These send a proper branded email once your email domain is connected. Until
   then, use the **Email** button to write from your own inbox.)
3. **Add a note.** Type anything — "Called back, keen, sending link" — and tap
   **Save note**. Every status change, note and reply is kept as a dated history
   at the bottom, so you always see the story of a lead, not just its latest state.

## Reviewing a brief (before a survey)

When a client finishes the portal brief, open their lead and tap **View brief**
(or open it from the inbox). You get everything you need to prep a survey:

- **Dimensions and working area** — worked out for you.
- **The written brief**, plus postcode, second spaces and access notes
  (gate widths, skip parking, shared drives — the awkward stuff).
- **Photos and video** — tap any to view full-screen; swipe/arrow between them.
- **Their layout sketch** — what they drew or photographed.
- **Inspiration** — links and images of the look they're after (links open in a new tab).

When you've been through it, tap **Mark reviewed & notify client**. That records
it as reviewed and emails the client to say you've seen their brief and to line
up the call.

---

## A few good habits

- **Act on gold.** Gold tags and buttons are the "do something" signal.
- **Advance as you go.** Keeping statuses current is what makes the dashboard honest.
- **Notes beat memory.** Ten seconds of note now saves a confused call later.
- **Watch "Out of area."** If that number keeps climbing, it's real evidence that
  a bigger radius — or a second base — would pay.

---

## If something looks wrong

- **Nothing loads / "not an admin":** your email may not be on the admin list yet,
  or your sign-in expired. Sign in again at **/admin**; if it persists, the owner
  re-runs the one-line admin step in `SETUP.md`.
- **Two people, one lead:** changes appear on the other person's screen when they
  refresh — it's the same live data, not separate copies.
- **Emails not sending:** the console still records everything; only the *emails*
  wait until the Resend key + domain are connected (see `SETUP.md`, sections 3 & 4).

That's the whole tool. Leads in, briefs reviewed, statuses moving — from your phone,
between jobs.
